Privacy Policy

Last updated: 15 July 2026

The short version: Reactor: Core Siege has no account and no servers. Your entire game — every run, turret, blueprint, upgrade and record — lives only on your device. We collect nothing about you: no analytics, no crash reports, no profile. The single thing that ever leaves your phone is the data Google AdMob needs to show the game's ads, and buying Ad-Free Forever removes the ads and that data flow entirely. Everything is described in full below.

What Core Siege accesses on your device

Nothing you have to grant. The game asks for no permissions at all — no photos, no contacts, no calendar, no microphone, no location, no health data. It does not need them and does not request them.

Your game data stays on your device

Your progress — descent history, the blueprint collection, prestige-tree upgrades, Sparks, ascension rung, Daily streak, best depth, and your settings — is stored locally on your iPhone using Apple's on-device storage. There is no Core Siege server, no cloud save, and no database anywhere that holds your game or your identity. If you delete the app, that data is deleted with it.

No account required

There is no sign-up, no email address, no phone number, and no social login. We never ask who you are, because the game never needs to know.

Analytics and crash reporting — the honest disclosure

We collect none. Core Siege sends us no usage events, no analytics, and no crash reports. We genuinely do not know how you play, how far you get, or whether you play at all. There is no telemetry in this app.

If that ever changes, this page will be updated before the change ships, and the change will be noted in the App Store release notes.

Advertising

Core Siege includes ads served by Google AdMob: optional rewarded ads (you choose to watch a short video in exchange for an in-game bonus — if you never tap that option, you never see one) and a limited, frequency-capped interstitial. This is the only part of the app that shares any data with anyone. Here is exactly what it involves:

  • What AdMob collects — to serve and measure ads, Google AdMob may collect and use your device's advertising identifier (IDFA) and related technical data (device type, coarse app-usage and ad-interaction signals, and approximate/IP-derived location). This is used to show ads, measure their performance, and limit how often you see the same ad.
  • Shared with Google as our ad provider — this data goes to Google, which acts as the advertising provider and, with its ad partners, may use it to serve ads. Google's list of ad technology partners is available here.
  • Your tracking choice (App Tracking Transparency) — iOS asks for your permission before the app uses your advertising identifier to track you across other companies' apps and websites. If you decline, you still get the game and the rewarded ads — the ads are simply non-personalized. You can change your answer any time in iOS Settings › Privacy & Security › Tracking.
  • Consent in the EEA/UK — if you are in the European Economic Area, the UK, or Switzerland, you are asked for consent before any personalized-ads data is used, via Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) consent prompt. You can decline, and you can change your choice later.
  • Ad-Free Forever removes it — the one-time Ad-Free Forever purchase removes all ads permanently. With it, the ad SDK is not used to serve you ads, and this entire data flow stops. At that point nothing leaves your device at all.

Google's use of the advertising data described above is governed by Google's advertising policy. You can reset or delete your advertising identifier at any time in your device's system settings.

Purchases

Core Siege offers three optional one-time purchases — a Starter Pack, Ad-Free Forever, and a Spark Pack. There is no subscription. All purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store using StoreKit. Core Siege never sees your payment method, Apple ID, or billing details. Apple's privacy policy governs that transaction.

Fair play — what your money cannot buy

Not strictly a privacy matter, but it is a promise worth writing down: the Daily Descent is wallet-immune. Every player gets the identical board for a given day, and no purchase — none of the three above — can change its outcome or your standing. Paying only speeds up your own private progress. Ranking is local for now.

Data we do not collect

To be explicit about what Core Siege does not do:

  • We do not have a server, so we cannot and do not store anything about you.
  • We do not collect analytics or usage events.
  • We do not collect crash reports.
  • We do not track your location.
  • We do not access your photos, contacts, calendar, microphone, or health data.
  • We do not use device fingerprinting. The only cross-app identifier involved is the device advertising identifier, used solely to serve the ads described in the Advertising section — and only if you allow it.
  • We do not sell your data.

Children

Core Siege is rated 9+ and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. The game's ads are not aimed at children.

Data breach risk

Because Core Siege stores nothing on any server, there is no Core Siege database to breach. Your game lives on your device, governed by Apple's device security model — we recommend a strong passcode and Face ID/Touch ID.

Retention

Your game data lives only on your device and is removed when you delete the app or reset your progress in-game. Advertising data handled by Google is retained under Google's own retention policy; we never receive or store a copy of it.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes we will update the date at the top of this page. Material changes will be noted in the App Store release notes. Continued use of Core Siege after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.
