StoryNest — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 3 July 2026 — StoryNest v1.0

App Store label: Data Not Collected
The short version: StoryNest has no backend, no account, and no analytics. Everything — your child's first name, their chosen hero, and their story progress — stays on your device. Nothing is transmitted, sold, or shared. There is no server to breach and no database to subpoena, because there is no server and no database. StoryNest works 100% offline.

1. What StoryNest stores (on your device only)

StoryNest saves a small amount of information locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework: the child profiles you create (an optional first name and an age band), the hero each child chooses (and the on-device hero name you give it), story progress (which stories were finished, which path was taken, where to resume), and your in-app settings (narration voice and speed, captions, reduce-motion, sleep-timer default). This data is stored on-device, is encrypted by iOS at rest, and is never sent to us or anyone else.

2. We collect nothing

StoryNest does not collect, transmit, or receive any personal data. We do not have a user account system, we do not ask for an email address, and we never see your child's name, hero name, or story activity. Apple's App Store privacy label for StoryNest is "Data Not Collected."

3. No analytics, no crash reporting SDKs

StoryNest contains no third-party analytics SDK (no Firebase, Amplitude, Mixpanel, Segment, or equivalent) and no third-party crash-reporting SDK (no Crashlytics, Sentry, or equivalent). No network request is ever made for analytics or observability. If iOS shows you Apple's standard crash-report dialog and you choose to share, that report goes to Apple under Apple's privacy policy; any aggregate crash data we may see in App Store Connect is anonymised and never identifies your child.

4. No advertising, no tracking

StoryNest shows no ads and contains no advertising SDK. It does not use the IDFA, the IDFV, or any device identifier, and it never presents an App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt — there is nothing to track.

5. No location, camera, microphone, or contacts

StoryNest does not request or access your location (precise or coarse), the camera, the microphone, your contacts, or your photos.

6. Offline by design; the only network use

StoryNest is usable entirely offline after install. The single exception is that, on a grown-up's explicit action (downloading additional story packs), the app fetches static illustration and audio files from a content host. These downloads carry no personal data, require no account, and are only asset files — nothing about your child or your device is sent in the request beyond what any file download requires.

7. Purchases

Optional purchases (the lifetime Family Unlock, or the optional annual/monthly plans) are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store using StoreKit 2. We never see your payment details, card number, or Apple ID email. Apple's privacy policy governs payment processing.

8. Parental gate

Anything that is not a story — purchases, settings, and links — sits behind a parental gate that a young child cannot trivially pass. Children cannot reach commerce or external links without an adult passing the gate.

9. Children's privacy (COPPA / GDPR-K)

StoryNest is a Kids Category app designed for ages 6–8. Because StoryNest collects no personal information from any user, no verifiable parental consent mechanism is triggered — there is simply nothing collected. The child's first name and hero name are entered by a grown-up during setup, stay on the device, and are never transmitted. We do not collect biometrics, persistent identifiers, or any other data subject to the COPPA Rule.

10. Data retention and deletion

Because we hold no data, there is nothing for us to delete on request. All data lives on your device. To remove it, delete the StoryNest app — iOS will remove all associated on-device storage.

11. Third-party services

StoryNest integrates with no third-party services beyond Apple's own frameworks (SwiftData, StoreKit, AVFoundation). Apple's own data handling is governed by the Apple Privacy Policy.

12. Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the date above will be updated and the change noted in the app's release notes. Any material change that increased data collection would require your explicit consent first — though we intend never to collect data at all.

13. Contact

Questions about this policy? Email dark2torch@gmail.com. We aim to respond within two business days.
