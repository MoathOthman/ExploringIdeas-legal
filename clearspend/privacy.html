Privacy Policy

Last updated: 30 June 2026 — ClearSpend v1.0

App Store label: Data Not Collected
The short version: ClearSpend has no backend and no account. Your transactions, budgets, categories, and wallets are stored only on your device — or in your own private iCloud account if you turn on sync. We never link to your bank, never see your numbers, and have no server to breach or subpoena. We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share anything.

1. What ClearSpend stores

Everything you enter — transaction amounts, categories, notes, wallets, budgets, and savings goals — is saved locally using Apple's SwiftData framework on your device. This storage is encrypted by iOS at rest (it requires your device passcode / Face ID / Touch ID to access). We never receive a copy of this data.

2. No bank linking, ever

ClearSpend is manual-first by design. It does not connect to your bank, does not use Plaid or any aggregation service, and never asks for bank credentials. You log spending by hand — including cash — so there is no financial-institution login for us, or anyone, to access.

3. iCloud sync (optional, opt-in, Pro)

If you enable iCloud sync, your data is synced through Apple's CloudKit private database. This is your own personal iCloud account; the data is end-to-end encrypted by Apple where the platform supports it and is accessible only to you on your signed-in devices. ClearSpend has no access to your CloudKit data — Apple's servers route the sync without exposing the content to us.

You can disable iCloud sync at any time in iOS Settings › Apple ID › iCloud › ClearSpend. Disabling sync does not delete local data.

4. Analytics and crash reporting

ClearSpend contains no analytics SDK (no Amplitude, Mixpanel, Firebase Analytics, Segment, or equivalent) and no crash reporting service (no Sentry, Crashlytics, or equivalent). Zero network requests are made for observability purposes.

If you choose to submit a crash report via Apple's standard iOS crash reporter dialog, that report goes to Apple and is governed by Apple's privacy policy. We may receive anonymised, aggregated crash data from Apple's App Store Connect dashboard; this never identifies you and contains none of your financial data.

5. Advertising

ClearSpend contains no advertising SDK and shows no ads. We do not use Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework because we have nothing to track.

6. Purchases

Optional Pro purchases (a one-time lifetime unlock or a yearly subscription) are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store using StoreKit 2. We never see your payment details, card number, or Apple ID email. Apple's privacy policy governs payment processing.

7. Notifications

If you grant notification permission, ClearSpend schedules local notifications on-device (e.g., budget alerts or a daily logging reminder). No notification content is sent to any server. Scheduling happens on your device only. You can revoke notification permission at any time in iOS Settings › Notifications › ClearSpend.

8. Device identifiers

ClearSpend does not read, store, or transmit any device identifier — not IDFA, not IDFV, not the device serial number. There is no identifier to associate with your data.

9. Children

ClearSpend is rated 4+ and is not directed at children under 13. It collects no personal data from any user.

10. Data retention and deletion

Because we hold no data, there is nothing for us to delete on request. All your data lives on your devices. To delete it:

  • On-device data: delete the ClearSpend app from your device. iOS will remove all associated SwiftData storage.
  • iCloud data: delete the app and also go to iOS Settings › Apple ID › iCloud › Manage Account Storage and delete ClearSpend's iCloud data.
  • Exported files: any CSV/JSON you exported lives wherever you saved it (Files, email, etc.) and is yours to manage.

11. Third-party services

ClearSpend integrates with no third-party services beyond Apple's own frameworks (SwiftData, CloudKit, StoreKit, UserNotifications). All of Apple's own data handling is governed by the Apple Privacy Policy.

12. Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the date above will be updated and the changes will be noted in the app's release notes. A material change that increases data collection would also require your explicit consent before taking effect — though we intend never to collect data at all.

13. Contact

Questions about this policy? Email dark2torch@gmail.com. We aim to respond within two business days.

A note on financial guidance

ClearSpend is a personal budgeting and expense-tracking tool for general awareness. It is not financial, investment, accounting, or tax advice, and is not a substitute for a qualified professional. For decisions specific to your situation, consult a licensed financial advisor or accountant.
