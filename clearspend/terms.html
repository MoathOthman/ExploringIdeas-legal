5. Shared Wallet: conduct and two-party data

A Shared Wallet is co-owned in practice by its two members. By using it you agree:

  • Both members can view, add, edit, and delete every record in the shared wallet. There is no per-record privacy inside a shared wallet — the privacy boundary is the wallet itself. Share only a wallet whose entire contents you are willing for the other member to see.
  • You are responsible for the data you contribute and confirm you have the right to share it. Do not put another person’s data into a shared wallet without their agreement.
  • Roles: the wallet owner can invite or remove the other member and can delete the shared wallet; either member can leave at any time. (Full role behavior is in the app and the Privacy Policy.)
  • Two-party data ownership & separation on exit (dispute / breakup). Because a shared wallet often involves a couple or household, exits are designed to be humane and to avoid holding anyone’s data hostage:
    What happensResult
    A member leaves The leaving member keeps a local personal copy of the shared records (it detaches into a normal personal wallet on their device). The cloud share continues for the remaining member.
    The owner leaves (a member remains) Ownership transfers to the remaining member. The former owner keeps a local personal copy.
    The owner deletes the shared wallet Both members are notified and offered an export/local copy; the cloud data is removed after a 30-day grace period.
    The Household subscription lapses The shared wallet becomes read-only and exportable — never deleted, never held hostage. Re-subscribing restores editing.
    Principle: a member can always walk away with their own local copy of any data they could see. We do not adjudicate disputes between members over shared content; the design instead guarantees each person can keep and export their copy.
  • Your personal wallets, budgets, and goals stay private and on-device; they are never shared, even if you also use a Shared Wallet.
